Methodology for Synthetic Clinical Nutrition Dataset Generation

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) Calculation

BMR was calculated using the Mifflin–St Jeor equation.

For males:
BMR = 10 × weight (kg) + 6.25 × height (cm) − 5 × age + 5

For females:
BMR = 10 × weight (kg) + 6.25 × height (cm) − 5 × age − 161

This equation is widely accepted in clinical nutrition practice.

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE)

TDEE was computed as: BMR × activity multiplier.

Activity multipliers used:

Sedentary: 1.20

Light: 1.375

Moderate: 1.55

Active: 1.725

This converts resting energy needs into total daily energy requirements.

Calorie Adjustments Based on Clinical Factors

Age > 30 years: subtract 5 kcal per additional year.

Steps > 10,000/day: increase calories up to 10%.

Hypertension (≥140/90 mmHg): reduce calories by 5%.

Weight loss goal: reduce 300 kcal/day.

Final calorie values restricted between 1200–3500 kcal/day.

Carbohydrate Ratio Determination

Base formula:
Carbohydrate ratio = 0.55 − (HbA1c − 6.5) × 0.04

Clipped within 25%–55% of total calories.

Additional carb reductions applied if:

Diabetes duration > 5 or 10 years

Fasting glucose > 130 mg/dL

Postprandial glucose > 180 mg/dL

Sleep < 6 hours

Smoking present

Alcohol use present

Primary goal is glycemic control

Protein Ratio Determination (Renal Safety Based)

eGFR ≥ 90 → 20% of calories

eGFR 60–89 → 18%

eGFR < 60 → 15%

Minimum protein safeguard:

0.8 g/kg body weight (normal kidney function)

0.6 g/kg body weight (renal impairment)

Fat Ratio Determination

Fat ratio calculated as:
Fat = 1 − carbohydrate ratio − protein ratio

Restricted within 20%–45% of total calories.

Fat reduced if:

Triglycerides >150 mg/dL (−5%)

Triglycerides >250 mg/dL (−10%)

LDL >130 mg/dL (−5%)

LDL >160 mg/dL (−10%)

Slight increase applied for female gender and alcohol use.

Ratios normalized if total exceeded 100%.

Conversion of Macronutrients to Grams

Carbohydrates: 4 kcal per gram

Protein: 4 kcal per gram

Fat: 9 kcal per gram

Formulas:

Carbohydrates (g) = Total calories × carb ratio / 4

Protein (g) = Total calories × protein ratio / 4

Fat (g) = Total calories × fat ratio / 9

Fiber Estimation

Fiber formula:
Fiber = 25
+ (steps / 2500)
+ (HbA1c − 6) × 2
+ 5 (if smoking)
+ 3 (if HDL < 40)

Additional +2 g if sleep > 7 hours.

Final fiber range restricted to 25–50 g/day.

Correlated Clinical Feature Sampling

HbA1c correlated with fasting and postprandial glucose.

BMI correlated with lipid profile and blood pressure.

Age correlated with blood pressure and eGFR decline.

Diabetes duration influenced renal function.

Physical activity level influenced step count.

This ensures realistic biological relationships in the dataset.

Output Targets Generated
Each synthetic patient record includes:

Daily calories (kcal)

Daily carbohydrates (g)

Daily protein (g)

Daily fat (g)

Daily fiber (g)

Final Summary
The dataset was generated using clinically validated metabolic equations and evidence-based medical nutrition therapy principles. Calorie needs were estimated using the Mifflin–St Jeor equation and adjusted using activity levels and health conditions. Macronutrients were dynamically tailored based on glycemic control, renal function, lipid profile, blood pressure, and lifestyle factors to ensure medical plausibility and physiological realism.
